# Chapter 02: Agile Mechanics & Characteristics

If the "Mindset" is the philosophy, the "Mechanics" are the recurring patterns found in almost every Agile team. Regardless of whether a team uses Scrum, Kanban, or XP, they generally share these ten characteristics and concepts.

---

## 2.1 Iterative & Incremental Progress
Agile teams do not build the whole car at once. They build a skateboard, then a scooter, then a bike, and finally a car.
* **Iterative:** We repeat the cycle of planning, building, and testing.
* **Incremental:** Each cycle adds a new, functional "slice" of value to the product.



---

## 2.2 The Minimum Viable Product (MVP)
The MVP is the smallest version of a product that allows a team to collect the maximum amount of validated learning about customers with the least effort. It isn't "broken" software; it is "focused" software.

---

## 2.3 The Backlog (The Source of Truth)
The Backlog is a prioritized list of everything that *might* be needed in the product.
* **Product Backlog:** The "Master List" managed by the Product Owner.
* **Refinement:** The ongoing process of "Grooming" the backlog—breaking big ideas into small, actionable User Stories.

---

## 2.4 Time-Boxed Iterations
Most Agile work happens in **Sprints**—fixed periods (usually 1–4 weeks). 
* The goal is to create a "Potentially Shippable Product Increment" by the end of every box.
* If a task isn't finished, it doesn't extend the sprint; it moves back to the backlog.

---

## 2.5 Transparency & Visibility
Agile only works if everyone can see the status of the system.
* **Information Radiators:** Tools like Kanban boards or Burndown charts that show exactly what is "In Progress" and what is "Blocked."
* **Daily Stand-Ups:** A 15-minute sync to ensure no one is working in a silo.

---

## 2.6 The Definition of Done (DoD)
The DoD is a shared agreement on what "finished" actually looks like. A feature isn't "Done" just because the code is written. A typical DoD includes:
* Code reviewed by a peer.
* Unit tests passed.
* Documentation updated.
* Deployed to a staging environment.

---

## 2.7 Continuous Delivery & Quality
Agile is often paired with **CI/CD** (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment). 
* We integrate code frequently to avoid "Integration Hell" at the end of a project.
* Quality is "built-in" through practices like **TDD** (Test-Driven Development) and regular code reviews.

---

## 🛠 Hands-on: Defining "Done"
Think about your current workflow. If you were to create a "Definition of Done" checklist for your next task, what would it include?
1. Does it include automated tests?
2. Does it include a security check?
3. Who has to "sign off" on it before it's actually considered finished?
