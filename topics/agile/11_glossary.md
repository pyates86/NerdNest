# Chapter 11: Agile Glossary

In high-performance engineering, vocabulary is a tool for precision. This expanded glossary defines the technical, philosophical, and structural terms used in modern Agile environments. Use this as your definitive "Header File" for professional discourse.

---

## A - C
* **Acceptance Criteria (AC):** The specific, testable conditions that a software product must meet to be accepted by a user or customer.
* **Agile Manifesto:** The foundational document (2001) outlining the 4 values and 12 principles of Agile.
* **Anti-Pattern:** A common response to a recurring problem that is usually ineffective and risks being highly counterproductive (e.g., "Scrum-but").
* **Backlog Grooming/Refinement:** The ongoing process of reviewing, splitting, and prioritizing backlog items to ensure they are "Ready."
* **Blocker / Impediment:** Anything that prevents a team member from progressing on a task.
* **Burndown Chart:** A graph showing work remaining (Y-axis) vs. time left in a sprint (X-axis).
* **Burnup Chart:** A graph showing total work completed vs. total work scope; excellent for visualizing "Scope Creep."
* **Capacity:** The total amount of labor (usually in hours or points) a team has available for a specific sprint.
* **CI/CD (Continuous Integration/Continuous Deployment):** Engineering practices for frequent code merging and automated release.
* **Cycle Time:** The time it takes for a task to go from "In Progress" to "Done."



---

## D - I
* **Daily Scrum / Stand-up:** A 15-minute daily synchronization for the developers to inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal.
* **Definition of Done (DoD):** A shared, standardized checklist of quality criteria (e.g., testing, linting, docs) that a story must meet to be complete.
* **Definition of Ready (DoR):** The criteria a story must meet before the team is allowed to pull it into a sprint (e.g., clear ACs, estimated).
* **Epic:** A large body of work that is too big to be completed in a single sprint and must be broken down into smaller User Stories.
* **Extreme Programming (XP):** An Agile framework focusing on technical excellence (TDD, Pair Programming, Simple Design).
* **Fibonacci Sequence:** A numbering scale (1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13...) used for relative estimation to reflect increasing uncertainty.
* **Icebox:** A storage area for stories or ideas that are not currently prioritized for the active Product Backlog.
* **Increment:** The sum of all completed backlog items that meet the DoD, representing a potentially shippable version of the product.
* **INVEST:** A mnemonic for high-quality user stories (Independent, Negotiable, Valuable, Estimable, Small, Testable).

---

## K - P
* **Kanban:** A visual system for managing work as it moves through a process, focusing on flow and WIP limits.
* **Lead Time:** The total time from when a request is made until it is delivered to the customer.
* **MVP (Minimum Viable Product):** The smallest version of a product that allows a team to gather maximum validated learning with the least effort.
* **Pair Programming:** Two developers working at one workstation (The Driver and The Navigator).
* **Planning Poker:** A consensus-based estimation technique used to avoid "Anchoring Bias."
* **Product Backlog:** An emergent, ordered list of what is needed to improve the product.
* **Product Owner (PO):** The role accountable for the "What"—maximizing product value and managing the backlog.

---

## R - S
* **Refactoring:** Improving the internal structure of code without changing its external behavior.
* **Relative Estimation:** Estimating the size of a task by comparing it to other tasks, rather than using absolute time (hours).
* **Release Train (ART):** A long-lived team of Agile teams that provides a continuous flow of releases in a Scaled Agile (SAFe) environment.
* **Retrospective:** A meeting held at the end of a sprint to inspect the team's process and identify improvements.
* **Scrum Master:** The servant-leader responsible for the "How"—promoting Scrum and removing team blockers.
* **Self-Organizing Team:** A team that chooses how best to accomplish their work, rather than being directed by others outside the team.
* **Spike:** A time-boxed story used for research, design, or technical exploration.
* **Sprint:** A fixed-length cycle (usually 1–4 weeks) where a team completes a set amount of work.
* **Sprint Goal:** A short, high-level statement that defines the purpose of the current sprint.
* **Story Points:** A unit of measure for expressing the overall effort required to fully implement a backlog item.

---

## T - Z
* **TDD (Test-Driven Development):** The "Red-Green-Refactor" cycle of writing tests before functional code.
* **Technical Debt:** The implied cost of additional rework caused by choosing an easy (but brittle) solution now instead of a better approach that would take longer.
* **Time-Boxing:** Setting a strict time limit for an event or task.
* **User Story:** A high-level description of a feature from the perspective of an end-user ("As a... I want... So that...").
* **Velocity:** The average number of story points a team completes per sprint.
* **Waterfall:** A linear, non-iterative project management model (Requirements -> Design -> Build -> Test -> Deploy).
* **WIP (Work in Progress):** The number of tasks currently being worked on but not yet finished.

You are now equipped to lead, execute, and deliver value in a modern engineering environment.
