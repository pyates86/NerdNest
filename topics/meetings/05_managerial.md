# Chapter 05: Managerial & Growth Meetings

Managerial meetings are often the most misunderstood part of an engineer's schedule. While project meetings move the code, **Managerial Meetings move the person.** These sessions are the primary interface between your individual goals and the organization’s objectives. If you treat these as "check-the-box" exercises, you are leaving your career growth to chance.

---

## 5.1 The 1:1 (One-on-One)
The 1:1 is the most important meeting on your calendar that isn't about code. It is **your** meeting, not your manager's. 

* **The Purpose:** To build a relationship, discuss "un-Slackable" topics, and provide a pulse check on your happiness and productivity.
* **The Engineer's Responsibility:** Come with an agenda. Don't wait for your manager to ask how you are doing. 
* **Topics to Cover:**
    * **Blockers:** Non-technical hurdles (process, people, or resources).
    * **Feedback:** Both giving it to your manager and asking for it.
    * **Ventilation:** Safely expressing frustrations before they lead to burnout.

---

## 5.2 The Performance Review
This is the formal "Audit" of your impact over a specific period (usually 6 or 12 months). 

* **The Self-Assessment:** This is your "Design Doc" for your career. Use data: "I reduced latency by 20%," not "I worked hard on the backend."
* **The Gap Analysis:** Identifying the delta between your current level and the next level (e.g., Mid-level to Senior).
* **No Surprises:** A healthy performance review should contain zero new information. If your manager tells you about a problem for the first time during a review, your 1:1s have failed.

---

## 5.3 Career Development & Coaching
While a 1:1 focuses on the *now*, a Coaching session focuses on the *future*. 

* **Skill Mapping:** Identifying which technical or soft skills you want to "level up."
* **Opportunity Alignment:** Asking for specific projects that align with your goals. (e.g., "I want to learn Kubernetes; can I shadow the DevOps lead on the next infrastructure migration?")
* **The Individual Development Plan (IDP):** A "backlog" for your professional growth.

---

## 5.4 Salary & Compensation Reviews
These are high-stakes meetings that require a shift from "Engineer Mode" to "Negotiator Mode."

* **The Evidence Folder:** Keep a "Brag Document" throughout the year. When it's time to talk money, you have a list of commits, PRs, and mentored peers to justify the increase.
* **Market Context:** Understanding where your skills sit in the broader industry.

---

## 5.5 Anti-Patterns to Watch For
* **The "Status Update" 1:1:** If you spend 30 minutes telling your manager what you did (which they can see in Jira), you have wasted the meeting. Use Slack for status; use 1:1s for human connection.
* **The Ghosting Manager:** If your manager consistently cancels 1:1s, they are signaling that your growth is not a priority. (Fix: Advocate for your time or escalate).

---

## 🛠 Hands-on: The 1:1 Agenda Refactor
For your next 1:1, try this "3-Item Agenda":
1.  **Top of Mind:** One thing that is currently worrying you or exciting you about the project.
2.  **The "Feedback Ask":** "What is one thing I could have done better in the last sprint?"
3.  **The "Future Look":** "I’d like to get more involved in [X]; what's the first step to making that happen?"
