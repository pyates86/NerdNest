# Chapter 08: Meeting Resources

Mastering the "Human API" of meetings requires looking beyond engineering and into the worlds of organizational psychology, professional facilitation, and leadership theory. This chapter provides the external dependencies—books, podcasts, and communities—needed to upgrade your meeting performance.

---

## 8.1 Foundational Reading
If you want to move from a participant to a world-class facilitator, start with these core texts:

* **The Art of Gathering** (Priya Parker): A masterclass in why we meet and how to create "transformative" sessions that actually achieve their purpose.
* **Death by Meeting** (Patrick Lencioni): A "leadership fable" that diagnoses why most meetings are boring and provides a framework for making them engaging.
* **Rituals for Virtual Meetings** (Kursat Ozenc & Glenn Fajardo): Essential for the modern remote engineer; provides specific protocols for building engagement over video.
* **Surviving the Whiteboard** (William Gant): While focused on interviews, this is the gold standard for learning how to communicate technical ideas visually in real-time.

---

## 8.2 Podcasts & Media
For ongoing insights into team dynamics and synchronization:

* **Soft Skills Engineering:** Search their archives for "Meetings"—they have excellent episodes on how to decline meetings politely and how to fix broken stand-ups.
* **The Look & Sound of Leadership:** Short, punchy episodes on executive presence and how to "command a room" (even a virtual one).
* **Miro’s "Work Life" Blog:** Deep dives into how high-performing engineering teams (like Spotify or Atlassian) use visual collaboration to speed up decisions.

---

## 8.3 Frameworks & Tooling Standards
Standardized protocols for specific meeting outcomes:

* **The liberating Structures (liberatingstructures.com):** A menu of 33 facilitation techniques to replace "The Presentation" or "The Open Discussion" with more productive interaction patterns (e.g., "1-2-4-All").
* **IDEO’s Brainstorming Rules:** The industry standard for creative "Jam" sessions.
* **The "Silent Meeting" Manifesto:** Read the Amazon or Square approach to "Narrative-driven" meetings, where the first 20 minutes are spent reading a document in silence.

---

## 8.4 Communities for Leaders
* **The LeadDev:** A community and conference series with a wealth of articles on managing "Meeting Load" for engineering managers.
* **Luma / Butter Communities:** Emerging spaces focused on the "Art of Facilitation" and keeping attendees engaged.

---

## 🛠 Hands-on: Building Your "Facilitator’s Backlog"
1.  **Identify your Weak Point:** Are you great at technical logic but bad at keeping people on track? Or are you good at tracking time but bad at visual communication?
2.  **Pick One Resource:** Select one book or podcast from the list above.
3.  **The "One-Week Implementation":** Apply one new facilitation technique (like "Silent Reading" or "1-2-4-All") to your next team sync.
