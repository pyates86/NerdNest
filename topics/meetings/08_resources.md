# Chapter 08: Meeting Resources

Facilitating a great meeting is a skill that can be systematized. Just as we use boilerplate code to speed up development, we can use "Meeting Boilerplates" to ensure we don't miss critical steps. This chapter provides the templates, checklists, and tools to help you transition from a passive participant to a high-impact facilitator.

---

## 8.1 The "Meeting Spec" Template
Use this template for every calendar invite you send. Copy and paste this into the description field.

> **[Meeting Title]**
>
> **Goal:** (e.g., Decide on the migration strategy for the legacy DB)
> **Required Prep:** (e.g., Read the attached Design Doc before joining)
>
> **Agenda:**
> 1. Review current constraints (5m)
> 2. Presentation of Option A vs Option B (10m)
> 3. Q&A and technical concerns (10m)
> 4. Final decision and owner assignment (5m)
>
> **Parking Lot:** (To be filled during the meeting)

---

## 8.2 The Facilitator’s Checklist
Before you hit "Start" on that Zoom or Teams call, run through this mental pre-flight check:

* [ ] **The "Why":** Can I state the desired outcome in 10 words or less?
* [ ] **The "Who":** Is there anyone here who doesn't have a "voice" in this decision? (If so, make them optional).
* [ ] **The "How":** Do I have a way to capture notes (Shared Doc, Whiteboard, or Scribe)?
* [ ] **The "Time":** Am I prepared to end 5 minutes early to give people a "bio-break" before their next session?

## 8.3 Digital Tooling for Engineers
Modern engineering teams use more than just video calls. Here is the recommended "Meeting Stack":

| Tool Type | Example | Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Whiteboarding** | Miro / FigJam | Architecture diagrams and brainstorming. |
| **Async Video** | Loom | Replacing "Update" meetings with a screen-share recording. |
| **Documentation** | Notion / Confluence | Persistent meeting notes and decision logs (ADRs). |
| **Polling** | Slido / Mentimeter | Quick anonymous consensus during town halls. |
| **Retro Tools** | EasyRetro / MetroRetro | Specific templates for "Start, Stop, Continue" sessions. |

## 8.4 Recommended Reading for Leaders
If you want to go deeper into the art of gathering, these resources are industry favorites:

* **"The Art of Gathering" by Priya Parker:** Focuses on why we meet and how to create transformative experiences.
* **"Death by Meeting" by Patrick Lencioni:** A fable-based approach to fixing meeting culture in organizations.
* **"Surviving the Whiteboard" (Blog/Article):** Techniques for thinking and drawing on your feet during technical interviews and architecture reviews.
* **Google's "Guide: Understand team effectiveness":** Data-driven insights into why psychological safety is the key to productive meetings.

---

## 8.5 The "Meeting Health" Audit
Once a month, teams should perform a "Garbage Collection" on their recurring meetings.
1.  **List all recurring invites.**
2.  **Assign a Value Score (1-10):** How much did this meeting help us ship code this month?
3.  **Delete or Pivot:** If a meeting scores below a 5, delete it for two weeks. If nobody misses it, it stays deleted.

## 🛠 Hands-on: Create Your Own Boilerplate
1.  **Create a "Meeting Template"** in your notes app or email client.
2.  **Add a "Bio-Break" Rule:** Set your default meeting duration from 30 minutes to 25 minutes, and from 60 minutes to 50 minutes.
3.  **The "No-Agenda" Clause:** Practice the polite decline. If you receive an invite with no agenda, reply: *"I'd love to attend, but I'm not sure how I can best contribute without an agenda. Could you share the goals for this session?"*
