# Chapter 09: The Meeting Glossary

In the high-performance engineering world, how we talk about our "synchronization events" matters. This glossary defines the common terms, slang, and mental models used to keep meetings efficient and professional. Use this as your reference for "Meeting Jargon."

---

## A - C
* **Action Item:** A specific, discrete task that results from a discussion. It must have a single owner and a deadline.
* **Ad-hoc Meeting:** A meeting called outside of the regular schedule, usually to address an immediate crisis or a sudden technical blocker.
* **Agenda:** The "Source Code" of the meeting. A pre-defined list of topics to be covered to ensure the session reaches its goal.
* **Bio-break:** A short pause in a long meeting (usually 5–10 minutes) for participants to use the restroom, grab water, or stretch.
* **Blocker:** A specific problem or dependency that prevents an engineer from making progress on a task.
* **Brainstorming:** A divergent thinking technique used to generate a large number of ideas for the solution to a problem.

## D - L
* **Decision Log:** A persistent record (often an ADR - Architecture Decision Record) of what was decided in a meeting, preventing the team from having the same argument twice.
* **Default to Async:** The cultural principle of attempting to solve a problem via documentation or Slack before resorting to a meeting.
* **Facilitator:** The person responsible for managing the meeting's flow, ensuring all voices are heard and the agenda is followed.
* **Hard Interrupt:** A sudden meeting or notification that forces an engineer to drop their current "mental model," incurring a high context-switching cost.
* **Latency (Meeting):** The time wasted during a meeting due to lack of preparation, technical issues, or off-topic discussion.

## M - R
* **Maker Time:** Large, uninterrupted blocks of time (usually 3–4 hours) required for deep engineering work.
* **Parking Lot:** A technique where off-topic (but important) ideas are recorded to be discussed later, preventing the current meeting from "drifting."
* **Post-Mortem:** A meeting held after an incident to analyze what happened and how to prevent it, ideally conducted "blamelessly."
* **Pulse Check:** A quick, high-level overview of a team's mood or a project's status.
* **Rabbit Holing:** The act of getting stuck on a minor, low-value technical detail while a larger, more important decision is waiting.

## S - Z
* **Sidebar:** A separate conversation (often between only two people) that happens after a meeting to dive into a specific detail that didn't require the whole group.
* **Status Update:** A "Shallow" meeting type where people report on progress. These are high-priority candidates for being replaced by Slack or email.
* **Steering Committee:** A high-level leadership group that makes "go/no-go" decisions on major project milestones.
* **Timeboxing:** Setting a strict time limit for a specific task or discussion topic. When the timer hits zero, the team must move to the next item.
* **TL;DR (Too Long; Didn't Read):** A brief summary at the top of meeting notes for stakeholders who couldn't attend.

---

## 🛠 Using this Glossary
Mastering this vocabulary allows you to "signal" seniority. Instead of saying, *"Hey, we are talking about something else now, let's go back,"* you say, *"Let's put that in the **Parking Lot** so we can respect the **Timebox** for this decision."* One sounds like a complaint; the other sounds like leadership.
