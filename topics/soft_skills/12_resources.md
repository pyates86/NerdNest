# Chapter 12: Soft Skills Resources

Becoming a high-impact engineer requires a commitment to continuous learning—not just in frameworks and languages, but in human systems and psychology. This chapter provides a curated "Dependency List" of books, podcasts, and frameworks that serve as the gold standard for professional development.

---

## 12.1 Foundational Books
If you were to build a library for your "Human OS," these would be the core modules:

* **Crucial Conversations** (Patterson, Grenny, et al.): The definitive guide for handling high-stakes communication.
* **Radical Candor** (Kim Scott): How to be a kick-ass boss (or peer) without losing your humanity. Essential for feedback loops.
* **Deep Work** (Cal Newport): The "documentation" for protecting your focus and achieving peak productivity.
* **The Phoenix Project** (Gene Kim et al.): A novel about IT and DevOps that perfectly illustrates technical conflict, accountability, and workflow optimization.
* **Extreme Ownership** (Jocko Willink): A look at accountability through the lens of Navy SEAL leadership, highly applicable to engineering "missions."

---

## 12.2 Podcasts & Media
For "background processing" during your commute or gym sessions:

* **Soft Skills Engineering:** A weekly advice podcast for software developers. Highly recommended for practical, real-world answers to "awkward" office situations.
* **The Engineering Leadership Podcast (Eclip):** Focuses on how top engineering leaders scale teams and build culture.
* **Manager Tools / Career Tools:** While not dev-specific, their "Basics" series on 1:1s and feedback is the industry standard.
* **Hidden Brain:** A deep dive into the unconscious patterns (bias, heuristics) that drive human behavior.

---

## 12.3 Mental Models & Frameworks
Quick-reference "Algorithms" for social interaction:

* **The SBI Model (Situation-Behavior-Impact):** Your go-to function for delivering clear, objective feedback.
* **The Five Whys:** The root-cause analysis tool for both technical bugs and team failures.
* **The Eisenhower Matrix:** A sorting algorithm for your tasks—distinguishing between "Urgent" and "Important."
* **The SCARF Model:** A brain-based model for collaborating with others by understanding social "threats" and "rewards."

---

## 12.4 Online Communities & Courses
* **LeadDev:** A community and conference series specifically for engineering leads and those who want to get there.
* **Pluralsight/LinkedIn Learning:** Search for "Professional Skills for Developers" paths.
* **Project Aristotle (Google):** Read the research summary on why "Psychological Safety" is the #1 predictor of team success.

---

## 🛠 Hands-on: Building Your "Backlog"
Don't try to consume all of these at once. Instead:
1.  **Pick one "Gap":** Identify which soft skill is currently your biggest bottleneck (e.g., giving feedback or managing time).
2.  **Select one Resource:** Choose one book or podcast from the list above that addresses that gap.
3.  **Implement one "Patch":** Commit to trying one technique from that resource in your work next week.
