# Chapter 13: The Soft Skills Glossary

In the tech industry, professional development has its own "jargon." Understanding these terms is essential for navigating performance reviews, leadership meetings, and team dynamics. This glossary serves as a quick-reference "Header File" for the concepts covered in this track.

---

## A - C
* **Accountability:** The obligation of an individual or organization to account for its activities, accept responsibility for them, and to disclose the results in a transparent manner.
* **Affinity Bias:** The unconscious tendency to gravitate toward people who share similar backgrounds, interests, or appearance.
* **Attention Residue:** The cognitive "lag" that occurs when switching tasks; the phenomenon where your brain is still processing the previous task while you are trying to focus on a new one.
* **BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front):** A communication technique where the most important information (the conclusion or request) is placed at the very beginning of the message.
* **Confirmation Bias:** The tendency to search for, interpret, and recall information in a way that confirms one's pre-existing beliefs or hypotheses.
* **Crucial Conversation:** A discussion between two or more people where stakes are high, opinions vary, and emotions run strong.

## D - L
* **Deep Work:** Professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit.
* **Disagree and Commit:** A management principle where individuals are allowed to disagree during the decision-making process, but once a decision is made, everyone must commit to its success.
* **Emotional Intelligence (EQ):** The ability to identify and manage one’s own emotions, as well as the emotions of others.
* **Five Whys:** An iterative interrogative technique used to explore the cause-and-effect relationships underlying a particular problem by repeating the question "Why?"
* **Human API:** A metaphor for how an individual interacts, communicates, and "interfaces" with teammates and stakeholders.

## M - R
* **Maker’s Schedule:** A schedule used by people who create things (engineers, writers, designers) that requires long, uninterrupted blocks of time to be productive.
* **Mentorship:** A professional relationship in which an experienced individual (mentor) guides a less experienced person (mentee) in developing specific skills and knowledge.
* **Psychological Safety:** The belief that one will not be punished or humiliated for speaking up with ideas, questions, concerns, or mistakes.
* **Radical Candor:** A management philosophy defined by the ability to "Challenge Directly" while simultaneously "Caring Personally."
* **Request for Changes (RFC):** In the context of a code review, a formal request for an author to modify their code before it is merged into the main branch.

## S - Z
* **SBI Model:** A framework for giving feedback that stands for Situation, Behavior, and Impact.
* **Social Technical Debt:** The hidden cost of poor communication, lack of trust, or unresolved conflict within a technical team that slows down development.
* **Sponsorship:** An active form of support where a leader uses their influence and "social capital" to advocate for an individual’s career advancement.
* **Unconscious Bias:** Social stereotypes about certain groups of people that individuals form outside their own conscious awareness.
* **Zero-Sum Thinking:** The incorrect belief that for one person to "win" or "be right," another person must "lose" or "be wrong."
